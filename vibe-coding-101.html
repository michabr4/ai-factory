What Is Vibe Coding?

Traditional Coding
  • ✗ Learn a programming language first
  • ✗ Type exact syntax the computer understands
  • ✗ Debug cryptic error messages alone
  • ✗ Months to build something useful
Vibe Coding
  • ✓ Describe what you want in plain English
  • ✓ AI writes the code for you
  • ✓ Conversational back-and-forth to refine
  • ✓ Working results in minutes to hours

In short: Vibe coding is building software by having a conversation with an AI assistant. You bring the what and the why. The AI handles the how.

Your First Session

Here's exactly what to do when you open a tool like Windsurf or Cursor for the first time.

1

Open the tool and find the chat panel

Every AI code editor has a chat window — usually on the right side. This is where you talk to the AI.

2

Describe what you want in plain English

Example: "Build me a single-page website that lists our team's 5 current projects with a status indicator for each one. Use a clean, modern design."

3

Review what the AI creates

The AI will generate code files. Click "Accept" to save them. Then preview in your browser (the tool usually offers a preview button or you open the file).

4

Give feedback, naturally

Say things like: "Make the font bigger", "Change the green to Cisco blue", "Add a column for project owner". Keep going until it looks right.

How to Talk to the AI

The quality of your results depends on how you describe what you want. Here are real examples.

Vague (harder for AI)

"Make me a website"

Specific (much better)

"Build a single-page dashboard showing 8 team roles with their descriptions. Use a dark header, card layout, and Cisco brand colors."

Vague

"Fix the colors"

Specific

"Change the header background from purple to Cisco blue (#049fd9) and make the subtitle text lighter."

Vague

"It's broken"

Specific

"When I click on the Service Delivery Manager card, it expands but the card next to it also shows an arrow. Only the clicked card should expand."

Pro tip: You can paste screenshots into the chat. Show the AI what's wrong visually — it understands images too.

Five Tips for Success

01

Start Small

Don't try to build an enterprise app on day one. Start with a simple page, a small tool, or a personal dashboard. Grow from there.

02

Be the Product Manager

Your job is to know what you want and clearly communicate it. The AI is your developer. Direct it like you would a teammate.

03

Iterate, Don't Restart

If something isn't right, tell the AI what to fix — don't start over. Each correction teaches the AI your preferences for the rest of the session.

04

Ask "Why" When Unsure

When the AI does something you don't understand, ask it to explain. It's a learning opportunity and ensures you stay in control of your project.

05

Read the Approvals

The AI will sometimes ask to run commands or make changes. Read what it's proposing before clicking "Accept." Most actions are safe — installing packages, creating files — but always read first. If it mentions deleting files or running something unfamiliar, ask it to explain.

Now Go Build Something

The best way to learn vibe coding is to start. Pick one small thing you wish existed — a tracker, a dashboard, a calculator — and describe it to the AI.

Starter Idea #1

"Build me a personal task board with three columns: To Do, In Progress, and Done. Let me drag tasks between columns."

Starter Idea #2

"Create a simple meeting notes page where I can type notes, tag action items, and see them listed at the bottom."

Starter Idea #3

"Make a team directory page with everyone's name, role, and photo. Include a search box to filter by name."
